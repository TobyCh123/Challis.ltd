About Ski Pass

Ski Pass is a board game for 2-6 players. Collecting cards, completing routes and dealing with tricky terrain. Players take turns travelling the mountains and conquering the slopes in an adventure to decide who is going to be the leader for your next snowy holiday.

  • A board familiar to any ski/snowboard goers
  • Simple instructions - tailor the complexity
  • Carefully designed game pieces and cards
  • Average game time: 60-90 minutes
  • Suitable for all ages

Read on to find out more...

The Basics The Slopes The Rules
Step 1: Setup

Are you a skier or a snowboard?
Either way, first step is pick your favourite playing piece and hand out your SkiRun cards. After that select your starting routes and head off.

Step 2: Gameplay

Play cards to Ski down slopes, go up lifts or cross between resorts. Keep going to complete your routes and master the slopes. If you want you can play with wildcards to mix up the game and keep players on their toes.

Step 3: Winning

Who wins? The fastest? The most skilled? The least falls?
No? The one with the most points!
Get points for skiing slopes and completing routes. The winner gets a free round of drinks during apres!

The board

The board is carefully crafted mountain side featuring slopes from green to black, off-piste sections and bus routes.
Do you try and complete long routes like Chinaillon to Cote 2000 or keep them short and sweet like Le Lachat to Le Grand Bornand

The Beginning

  • Give out 5 SkiRun Cards per player
  • Give out 5 SkiRoute Cards per player (they may discard up to 2)
  • Each player chooses if they are a skier or snowboarder
  • If playing Full SkiDay - Give out 5 SkiDay Cards

The player who fell over most recently on a ski trip starts and play continues clockwise
On each go a player can either:

  • Pick up 2 SkiRun cards or 1 Snowboard Card
  • Lay one run
  • Pick up some SkiRoute cards

Skiers vs Snowboards

At the Beginning of the game the players must choose if they are a skier or a snowboarder (aiming for an even split). Some SkiDay cards only affect one group, as indicated on the card.
There are also certain runs on the board marked with a set of skies or a snowboard, for these runs a player must lay the run as usual plus lay one extra GoggleCard if the symbol matches their ski/snowboard status

Cards

SkiRun

The SkiRun cards equate to the run colours on the board (Green, Blue, Red, Black, Grey, 'Chair Lift'). In order to claim a run you must have the correct number of the correct colour to handover on your go.

GoggleCards represent all colours and can be used on any run to make up numbres if you do not have the correct number of the correct colour.

In order to 'Go Off-Piste (claim a white run) any colour card can be used, you can handover the correct number of cards of one colour.

SkiRoute

These are the routes that a player must complete in order to earn points. The points to be gained from completing that route is shown on the card.

If a player does not complete that route then that point amount is deducted from their score at the end.

At the beginning of a game a player is given 5 SkiRoute Cards, they must keep a minimum of 3 and can choose to discard or keep the remaining.

A player may then select more route cards on any of their goes throughout the game. In this instance they are given 3 card and they can discard up to 2.

SkiDay - Optional

At the beginning of a game each player is given 5 SkiDay cards.
These cards fall into two catergories:

  • Avant: For use on that players go, at the start of their go they can play a card that will impact their go and in some cases the following round(s)
  • Apres: For use at the end of the game; once scores are being tallied, players may play appropiate cards to increase their score
Cards in a player's possession do not have to be used, they will just be wasted at the end of the game.

Bus Runs

There are bus runs to travel between the resort villages; Chinaillon, Le Grand Bornand, St Jean De Sixt and Le Clusaz.
In order to claim a bus route you must hand over 1 SkiRun card of each colour plus a GoggleCard for each bus symbol on the run. You only need 1 ski piece for a bus route, when you claim a route sit it on a bus. When all the buses are claimed the run is full. You gain 12 points for 1-bus runs and 16 points for 3-bus runs.

Scoring

For each run laid you gain points, these points are indicated on the board and increase with the length of the run claimed.

Keep track throughout the game, moving your board pieces as runs are claimed or count it up at the end.

Ending the Game

When a player lays ski pieces taking them under 3 ski pieces remaining that begins the last round, each player has one more turn (including the player who triggered the last round).
After gameplay ends take it in turns to calculate points earned from runs laid (if you haven't done this as you go), and then from routes completed.
If playing Full SkiDay version then also play any final cards of these if wanted.
Winning: The game winner is whoever has the most points at the end of the game. Congrats, and claim your 'Challis' of Grimbergen
